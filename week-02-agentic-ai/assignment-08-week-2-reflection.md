Week 2 of the DevOps Micro Internship took me deep into Agentic AI with Claude Code, and this week the biggest lessons came from things breaking.
I built AI Skills, configured specialized Subagents, connected Claude to GitHub through MCP, and set up Hooks and Permissions so the agent would refuse destructive commands before running them. But the real learning happened when my hook scripts kept failing with "No such file or directory." Slowing down, reading the error properly, and fixing the path resolution taught me more than any tutorial could.
My biggest takeaway: you do not trust automation blindly, you design the guardrails that make it safe to trust. Least privilege, safety by design, and systems that persist across sessions. These are not just AI ideas, they are the same disciplines I am building into my own work.
One habit I am keeping: define an agent's permissions and guardrails first, before letting it act on anything real.
